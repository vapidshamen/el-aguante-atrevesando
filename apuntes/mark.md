# mark

wow, so think about if you were [jesus of gamala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joshua_ben_Gamla) and then all of a sudden the book of mark comes out with a guy in it called jesus (same name as me) and in chapter 11 he has his grand entrance into jerusalem, but he's hungry for fruit from a fig tree (a fig looks like a scrotem hanging below a peice of wood) -- but it doesn't have any fruit (the galileans were castrated so they didn't have any figs hanging below their wood) -- so he curses the tree without figs/scrotems it withers and dies. proudly jesus says that if you have faith, whatever you believe will happen will happen (something almost certainly jesus of gamala said over and over, essentially to prove and establish the power of his words). then in chapter 12, jesus of gamala is vindicated as the beloved son of the creator of the walled vinyard (eden) -- yet in the parable he's killed by the "earth/soil workers" and thrown out of the vineyard/eden. the author of mark (who I believe is likely a colab between titus and josephus/paul) then turns jesus of gamala into the "chief cornerstone" whom the soil workers rejected (obviously of which titus was one of them). 

later on in chapter 15 jesus is delivered unto pilate -- a guy that disappeared on his way to rome 33 years ago to be put to death -- jesus is narrated to no longer judge (go silent) so as to amaze pilate -- and strangely enough the at the feast to discharge/release one of the prisoners, "so as to amaze pilate, down of the feast he [not sure he refers to pilate or jesus] would release/discharge to them one bound/prisoner which they would excuse/pardon in the one called barabbas [son of the father] -- with the uprising having been betrothed/bound, who in the uprising had committed unjustified homicide" -- then pilate asks them if they want to release to "king of the jews"
	"king of the jews" was a title given to [herod the great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herod_the_Great), the grandfather of [harod agrippa 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herod_Agrippa), the brother/wife of [berenice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berenice_(daughter_of_Herod_Agrippa)) (who was also the wife of [titus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titus) (which I also believe was the primary author of "mark" and destroyed the temple just as the book of mark prophesies))]
and yet the crowd picked barabbas (son of the father) over jesus
	it's almost as if the author of mark is telling jesus (who gives me the impression that he was not a very humble guy) -- that the populus wants a "son of the father" -- not some arrogant inciter of uprisings.
-- even though pilate knows that it was the chief priests (ie, [ananas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ananus_ben_Ananus)) who envied jesus and wanted him killed.
	jesus of gamala also was one of the high priests, but he became the high priest, mostly because the woman he was betrothed to and married ([martha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martha_daughter_of_Boethus)) was very rich, and according to the talmud, martha bribed the king with a whole lot of gold to make him high priest -- and [agrippa 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herod_Agrippa_II) appointed him as high priest. jesus was succeded by [matthias ben theophilus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mattathias_ben_Theophilus) -- who possibly is the matthias referred to in acts 1:21 -- and potentially the recipient of the works of luke/acts.

I just can't imagine what that would be like to be jesus of gamala and to read the book of mark around 69AD. I mean, talk about genius -- the author of mark essentially completely derails jesus' life trajectory to become (eventually) the emperor of rome. and the super crazy thing is -- jesus (who probably isn't thinking about dying, probably thinking himself to be invincible) now is narrated to rebuild his sanctuary in 3 days: "the one down-loosening the sanctuary and abode-building in 3 days, save yourself" [the word is καταλύω down+loosening (lol) -- and λύω is same word for "divorce" -- as in, being married is to be "bound" to a woman -- and then upon divorce, they are unbound/released]
-- and then low and behold titus crucifies jesus, and I wonder if by this time jesus is starting to believe the whole thing, so he just has to endure 3 days just like the prophecy says... and then after 3 days josephus sees him nailed to the cross and appeals to rome (titus) to be taken down from the cross... I guess he didn't save himself. I'm telling you, if I were jesus, I would be totally mind blown by the narrative genius and war of endurance that titus fought to, which was able to take me down. I would be honestly so impressed, titus would probably have become a good fried of mine after that (we know he was already an intimate friend of josephus)
